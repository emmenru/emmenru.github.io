ABOUT

Emma Frid received her PhD in Sound and Music Computing in January 2020. Her thesis was entitled Diverse Sounds - Enabling Inclusive Sonic Interaction and focused on how Sonic Interaction Design can be used to promote inclusion and diversity in music-making. Emma is the author of the first full review on Accessible Digital Musical Instruments (ADMIs). Her main research interests include inclusive music technology and practice, multisensory perception, sonic interaction design, multimodal interfaces and haptics. Emma holds a Master’s of Science in Engineering in Media Technology from KTH Royal Institute of Technology and has been a graduate research trainee at Input Devices and Music Interaction Laboratory (IDMIL), McGill University, where she completed a Master’s Thesis project on vibrotactile feedback for live electronics. Emma has also worked as an Audio Research Intern at the Adobe Creative Intelligence Lab in San Francisco, where she worked on interfaces supporting AI Music generation. When Emma is not working on music tech or sound and music research, she is playing the violin and piano and takes pictures.
